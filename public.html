Renovate Sr. Judges Chambers, GSA

The new 4000 sf suite includes the Judge's chambers, private restroom, Judge’s assistant’s office, reception area, galley, conference room, and two law clerk offices. The location of the chambers on the 2nd floor required relocation of the Grand Jury room. Due to the space limitations of this historic building, this function had to be combined with the Jury Selection as well as the Training Room functions, all in the same room. design2 LAST came up with three different room layouts to accommodate the three functions, including an innovative custom cabinet to hold a portable ramp, which doubles as a divider wall, between the raised dais and the seating area. The second floor corridor was restored to its original historic condition including removing carpet and restoring original terrazzo and marble floor finishes, repainting, removing and replacing 2x2 light fixtures with historically correct replicas. The third floor improvements include dividing the library into two spaces; an office and an attorney’s small conference room. Scope included replacement of the existing mechanical systems, upgrades of the electrical, communication, security and fire alarm systems, plumbing changes, and upgrades of the lighting in the second and third floor areas of work. Special considerations included historic preservation protocol, extremely sensitive sound isolation and privacy to preserve attorney/client privilege, and adding an ADA accessible toilet to the jury selection room. This project was completed using Revit to create the second and third floors in 3D modeling.
